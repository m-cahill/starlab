# STARLAB

## Moonshot Charter

**Strategic Testing, Analysis, Replay, and Learning Lab**

**Purpose:** This document is a transferable moonshot brief for seeding STARLAB in another project or repository. It is intentionally detailed on thesis, scope, milestones, research posture, and value targets, while avoiding system-specific architectural discussion. 

---

## One-line Thesis

**STARLAB is a reproducible, replay-native StarCraft II research substrate intended to transform a historically brittle game environment into a governed experimental surface for hierarchical, perception-grounded, multi-agent research.**

---

## Design Philosophy

STARLAB treats RTS research as a **systems problem first**, and an **agent problem second**.

---

## Working Posture

* Lab-first
* Benchmark-first
* Evidence-first

---

# Executive Summary

STARLAB is envisioned as a **flagship research program**, not a single-agent project.

Its role is to create a:

* Stable
* Replay-native
* Reproducible

lab surface for studying:

* Long-horizon decision-making
* Partial observability
* Hierarchical control
* Multi-agent dynamics

The core opportunity is **not just building a strong agent**, but creating a **governed research substrate** that makes RTS research:

* Inspectable
* Comparable
* Auditable
* Transferable

# Problem Statement

RTS research is both:

* Extremely powerful
* Extremely difficult

The issue is not just complexity — it is the lack of a **modern governed substrate** that enables:

* Stable experiments
* Replay-first workflows
* Portable evidence
* Comparable evaluation

**STARLAB solves this systems problem.**

---

# Why StarCraft II

StarCraft II uniquely combines:

* Partial observability
* Long-horizon planning
* Macro + micro control
* Adversarial dynamics
* Real-time pressure
* Rich replay data

This makes it the ideal **first proving ground**.

---

# Why Now

Modern agent research is shifting toward:

* Hierarchical systems
* Multimodal state representations
* Replay-based evaluation
* Reproducibility

STARLAB is positioned as a **2026-era lab** built for this shift.

---

# Design Philosophy (Expanded)

Priorities:

* Substrate credibility > model ambition
* Benchmark integrity > leaderboard chasing
* Evidence surfaces > ad hoc experimentation

The SC2 client should be treated as:

> An **untrusted boundary** from a program-management perspective

---

# Non-Goals

STARLAB is **not**:

* A ladder-first bot
* A monolithic super-agent project
* A modding platform
* A consumer product

It is a **research substrate first**.

---

# Program Objectives

* Create a **replay-native research surface**

* Bind:
  
  * runs
  * replays
  * evaluation
  * evidence

* Establish a **credible benchmark posture**

* Support multiple agent types:
  
  * Scripted
  * Heuristic
  * Imitation
  * Hierarchical
  * RL (later)

* Enable future **multi-environment expansion**
